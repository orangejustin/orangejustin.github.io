Zecheng Li (Justin)

I am a final year undergraduate student pursuing a BS in Mathematics-Economics in the Department of Economics and Cognitive Science with a specialization in Machine Learning in the Department of Cognitive Science. I am currently interested in the fields of machine learning, analytics, and econometrics. I am a data-driven person who never makes assumptions unless proven by data.

I am a research assistant (starting in January 2022) under the supervision of Prof Michael Davidson in the Davidson's Lab. My research area is the transition of Chinese state-owned coal companies to carbon neutrality and labor resettlement issues based on The Consequences of the Thermal Coal Phase-out in China.

The best way to learn is to teach. Due to my interest in machine learning, I am now a teaching assistant for a hardcore upper unsupervised machine learning class (Cogs 118b), helping students during my office hours and through Piazza.

This website is under reconstruction and lots of research projects and teachings have not been updated. I will delete this paragraph when they are up to date.
