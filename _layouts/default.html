Zecheng Li (Justin)

I am a final year undergraduate student pursuing a BS in Mathematics-Economics at the Department of Economics and a BS in Cognitive Science with a specialization in Machine Learning at the Department of Cognitive Science, and a minor in Data Science at the Halicioglu Data Science Institute. I am currently interested in the fields of machine learning, analytics, and econometrics. I am a data-driven person who never makes assumptions unless proven by data.

As an environmentalist with a strong passion for low-carbon transitions, I am currently a research assistant at Davidson's Lab, under the supervision of Prof. Michael Davidson, since January 2022. My research areas focus on the transition of Chinese state-owned coal companies to carbon neutrality and labor resettlement issues resulting from The Consequences of the Thermal Coal Phase-out in China. With my background in data analytics, I aim to use the power of data to solve climate problems and help drive the global transition to a more sustainable future.

The best way to learn is to teach, and that's why I served as a teaching assistant for (Cogs 118b),, an upper-level unsupervised machine learning class at UCSD. I provided one-on-one support to students during weekly office hours and discussion sessions, which allowed me to deepen my understanding of machine learning while helping others succeed in the course. I received "Strongly Agree" ratings on course evaluations for my helpfulness in explaining complex concepts and assisting with projects.

I wish more people had the opportunity to get to know me through this site. Unfortunately, due to ongoing processes or privacy issues, there are still many projects that I would like to include but cannot at this time.
